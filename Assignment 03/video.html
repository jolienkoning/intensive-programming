At first I didn't understand the meaning of the before and after pseudo element.

You choose in CSS before or/and after which element you want to add something. For example, when you need a dollar sign in front of every sentence, or a music note.

The point is that it will be in front of every element in which you want it to be.

Right now I've used a lot of different paragraphs, and put a BEFORE and AFTER on every paragraph.

You can choose the design you want!

One more important thing: the before and after pseudo adds adds text before and after an element without adding value to the content in the HTML.
